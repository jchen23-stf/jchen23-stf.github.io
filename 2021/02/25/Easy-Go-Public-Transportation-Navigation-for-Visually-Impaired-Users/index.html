Easy Go: Public Transportation Navigation for Visually-Impaired Users
Created 2021-02-25| Updated 2021-12-25
Post View:

TL;DR

This is a hackathon project for accessibility design. Our team made a voice-over friendly Android app for public transportation navigation. It was adopted and under a trial run by Yijiayi (http://www.yijiayi.org.cn/). Below is my notes during the prototyping stage, where we were doing brainstorming, user interviews, and feature design.

User pain points and core needs

  • Linear layout. Since visually-impaired people have to use voice-over to read from their screens. The best UI for them is a linear layoue like a menu, so they just scoll through and access all the information. But our existing navigation apps are not built for them - as you can see, they are very crowded and have a lot of pop-up tabs. It would be extremely difficult for visually impaired users to access information in this way.
  • Clear guidance for getting onto the bus. That means a way that is both safe and effective.
  • Shortcuts for most visited places. After interviewing them I realize that most of their lives are pretty linear between home, work, and maybe supermarkets and hospitals. They don’t have the luxury to explore different parts of the city spontaneously, but they do have the need to travel home from where ever they are.
  • Fast loading and minimal accidental crashing. Since we are offering real-time navigation for visually impaired users, quick loading time and minimal accidental crashing become especially important because any of those could get them into uncomfortable and even dangerous situation.
  • Accuracy - since the public transportation system is so unprediable, and you probably have already experienced this living in the bay area. We want to provide our users with the most-up to date and accurate information.
